For this homework, I displayed the world map in nine different projections. I used WGS84, the Aitoff Projection, EPSG: 3857, 53018, 54034, 54027, 102016, and I chose _______ and ______ for my additional two projections.

The Earth is a a three dimensional object, so all two dimensional maps have distortion in at least one of the following categories: shape, area, distance, and direction. No projection is perfect, so for this homework assignment, I looked at different world projections to see how they ehibited distortion of the Earth's surface.

WGS84 Projection

My observations: This projection has distortion of area and shape at the poles, while the areas nearest the equator have the least distortion.

Aitoff Projection

My observations: This projection shows distortion of shape, area, distance and direction. My observation leads me to think this projection is better suited for maps at a smaller scale than it is for maps of entire countries, let alone the entire world.

Pseudo-Mercator Projection

My observations: This map has distortion of area, esspecially near the poles. An example can be seen in Greenland, which has an exaggerated area. However, there is not distortion of direction.

Winkel Projection

My observations: This projection has distortion of shape, area and direction. This projection is better for world maps than small scale/local maps.

Equal Area Cylindrical Projection

My observations: This projection has distortion of shape, area and direction. The distortion increases as the distance from the equator increases.

Equidistant Conic Projection

My observations: Since this is a conic projection, it uses standard parallels. So, distortion of shape and area increases as distance from the standard parallels increases.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

My observations: In this projection, the distortion of shape and area increases as distance from the center point increases.

Bonne Projection

My observations: I chose this projection because it was a unique, heart shape. The Bonne Projection distorts shape, distance and direction.
